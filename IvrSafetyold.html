Overview

As part of my MA in Game Design at Falmouth University, I research learning through gamifcation so I looked into "Enhancing Learning through Gamification: Applying Goal-Setting Theory in IVR" for my final major project. This research-driven initiative explores the intersection of immersive virtual reality (IVR), gamification, and educational theory to revolutionize site safety training.

To answer this, I designed and implemented a gamified IVR training module that incorporates clear, attainable goals to intrinsically motivate learners. The prototype simulates various workplace scenarios, allowing users to interact with potential hazards in a safe, controlled environment.

Research Results

Key Findings

The research demonstrated several promising outcomes for VR-based safety training:

  • High Realism Score: Participants rated the simulation's realism and practicality at 5.63 out of 7, indicating strong potential for real-world skill transfer.
  • Strong Immersion: The experience achieved an immersion rating of 5.50 out of 7, suggesting effective learner engagement.
  • Effective for Kinaesthetic Learners: The study revealed particularly positive responses from hands-on learners, suggesting VR training may be especially beneficial for practical skill development.
  • Goal-Setting Integration: Immediate feedback mechanisms scored 5 out of 7, supporting the effectiveness of our goal-setting theory implementation.

Future Research Opportunities

While initial results are promising, several areas warrant further investigation:

  • Long-term knowledge retention and real-world skill transfer
  • Adaptive learning systems that respond to individual learning styles
  • Integration with existing training frameworks
  • Large-scale implementation studies across different industries
